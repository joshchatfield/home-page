H.E. Dzogchen Khenpo Choga Rinpoche
Buddhist Meditation Master and Authentic Dzogchen Lineage Holder

A Brief History

  • Rinpoche attended the Dzogchen Shri Singha University in Tibet
  • He graduated at the amazingly young age of 21 years old
  • He completed his seven years solitary cave retreat in the Dzogchen Valley
  • He was given the prestigious title of "Dzogchen Lineage Holder" by his teachers, who are lineage holders themselves
  • His teachers encouraged him to travel to the US in order to teach Buddhism in English

    • Beginning in 2000

  • He traveled around the US, teaching, picking up students and learning English
  • The Dzogchen Shri Singha Foundation, USA was founded in order to support Rinpoche's activities

    • Beginning in 2008

  • The DSSF purchased a 100+ acre retreat land
  • The retreat land is now thriving and hosts multiple events each year
  • Rinpoche's main activities now consitst of editing his books in English with the help of his students, and training teachers who have committed themselves to the retreat center

Pith Teachings

Positive thinking is to be adopted and negative thinking is to be abandoned. Positive thinking is: renunciation, compassion, faith, love, and wisdom. Negative thinking is: anger, jealousy, greed, pride, and ignorance.
Renunciation is reducing one's own negative thinking. Compassion is reducing other's negative thinking. Faith is recognizing positive thinking as the cause of one's own happiness. Love is recognizing other's positive thinking as the cause of their happiness and wanting it to increase. Wisdom is clearly seeing reality as it is.
Anger is wanting other's to suffer. Jealousy is liking other's suffering and not liking their happiness. Greed is identifying anything besides one's own positive thinking as the cause of happiness. Pride is comparing yourself to others in any way that keeps you from improving through renouncing your negative thinking. Ignorance is not seeing reality clearly.
